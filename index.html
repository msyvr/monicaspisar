In the lab: software experiments

2021/11: A ray tracing engine I wrote during my time at The Recurse Center*.

Digging into how photo-realistic images are created for movies and games, the key take away from my research ultimately didn't anchor on algorithms for modeling optical interactions but on the sophisticated hardware (supported by machine learning) that's been developed in recent years to support the inherent computational complexity. The ray tracing renderer I wrote is basic in the sense of not being tailored to leverage GPU capabilities, yet gave me an appreciation for the impact of cutting edge research in this space.

Realistic images at high frame rates for games rely on innovative approaches stitched together to meet stringent technical constraints, and exquisite animations and special effects in movies are made possible by a combination of advanced hardware and software supported by dedicated server farms. And both applications depend on sophisticated GPU platforms optimized for ray tracing.
